How should we programmatically extract and place graphics?

Claude prompts alone are failing to capture accurate bounding boxes for logos, shapes, and separators. Here are three programmatic approaches we can research and implement to hit your 95% accuracy goal.

A

Geometric / Spatial Logic (Heuristics)

Analyze the text blocks (which are highly accurate) and calculate where separators should logically exist. For example, insert a separator in the large vertical gaps between "Category Headers".

Pros

  • Fast & predictable
  • Doesn't rely on image noise

Cons

  • Might place separators where none exist visually
  • Hard to guess logo positions
B

Pure Computer Vision (OpenCV)

Use classic CV algorithms (Hough Lines, Contours, Template Matching) directly on the menu image to detect every line, circle, and shape, completely ignoring Claude for graphics.

Pros

  • Finds actual pixels
  • Highly accurate for straight lines

Cons

  • Background textures/noise cause false positives
  • Doesn't know if a shape is a separator or part of a logo
C

Hybrid: OpenCV + Semantic Filtering (Recommended)

Use OpenCV to find ALL physical shapes/lines on the menu. Then, use the highly accurate text bounding boxes to validate them. (e.g., A line found between a Category Header and its items is a valid separator; a shape near the top is a logo).

Pros

  • Highest potential accuracy (95%+)
  • Marries physical reality with logical context

Cons

  • More complex to build and tune
